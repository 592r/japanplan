15-Day Itinerary: Kyushu, Southern Honshu & Kyoto

Arrival Date: Suggesting April 7th, 2026 (Tuesday)

Day 1: Arrival in Fukuoka & Hakata Exploration (April 7th, Tuesday)

  • Morning/Afternoon: Arrive at Fukuoka Airport (FUK). Clear customs, purchase IC cards. Take the subway to Hakata Station. Check into your accommodation.
  • Afternoon: Explore the Hakata area. Visit Hakata Station City for shopping and dining. Stroll through Canal City Hakata, a large shopping and entertainment complex.
  • Evening: Experience Fukuoka's famous Yatai (food stalls) along the Nakasu River.
  • Accommodation Options (per night for 2 people):
    • Cheap (AUD $70-100): Hostel World Fukuoka, WeBase Hakata, The Life Hostel & Bar.
    • Normal (AUD $150-250): Hotel Forza Hakata, Dormy Inn Premium Hakata Canal City, JR Kyushu Hotel Blossom Hakata Chuo.
    • Pricy (AUD $300-500+): Grand Hyatt Fukuoka, Hotel Okura Fukuoka, The Royal Park Hotel Fukuoka.
  • Dinner Options (per person):
    • Cheap (AUD $10-20): Yatai stalls (Hakata Ramen, Yakitori), Shin Shin Hakata Ramen, Hakata Issou (ramen).
    • Normal (AUD $20-50): Hakata Hanamidori (Mizutaki), Hakata Motsunabe Yamanaka (Motsunabe), Umeyama Teppei (Teishoku, Goma Saba).
    • Pricy (AUD $50-100+): Wagyuryori Koshitsu Teppanyaki Zuisho Haruyoshi Branch (Wagyu Teppanyaki), Tatsumi Sushi (creative sushi).

Day 2: Fukuoka Culture & Gardens (April 8th, Wednesday)

  • Morning: Visit Dazaifu Tenmangu Shrine, a beautiful shrine dedicated to the god of learning, surrounded by plum and cherry trees. Explore the charming town around it.
  • Afternoon: Return to Fukuoka. Visit Ohori Park, a large park with a beautiful pond, perfect for a leisurely stroll. Nearby, explore the Fukuoka Castle Ruins in Maizuru Park (check for any lingering late cherry blossoms).
  • Evening: Enjoy dinner in the Tenjin area, known for its shopping and diverse dining scene.

Day 3: Travel to Nagasaki & Peace Memorial (April 9th, Thursday)

  • Morning: Check out of your Fukuoka accommodation. Take a train from Hakata Station to Nagasaki Station (approx. 2 hours). Check into your Nagasaki accommodation.
  • Afternoon: Visit the Nagasaki Peace Park and Atomic Bomb Museum. This is a somber but essential experience to understand Nagasaki's history.
  • Evening: Enjoy dinner in the Shinchi Chinatown area.
  • Accommodation Options (per night for 2 people):
    • Cheap (AUD $60-90): Casa Blanca Guesthouse, Hostel Casa Noda, APA Hotel Nagasaki Dejima.
    • Normal (AUD $120-200): Dormy Inn Premium Nagasaki Ekimae, Hotel Forza Nagasaki, Hotel Belleview Nagasaki Dejima.
    • Pricy (AUD $250-400+): Hotel New Nagasaki, Hilton Nagasaki, Garden Terrace Nagasaki Hotels & Resorts.
  • Dinner Options (per person):
    • Cheap (AUD $10-20): Local eateries for Champon, Sara Udon, or Kakuni Manju in Shinchi Chinatown.
    • Normal (AUD $20-50): Restaurants specializing in Turkish Rice, more established Champon/Sara Udon restaurants.
    • Pricy (AUD $50-100+): Upscale restaurants in Dejima Wharf with harbor views, specialty restaurants for refined local dishes.

Day 4: Nagasaki History & Views (April 10th, Friday)

  • Morning: Explore Glover Garden, an open-air museum with Western-style residences and stunning views of Nagasaki Harbor.
  • Afternoon: Visit Dejima, the historic artificial island, to learn about Japan's period of isolation and trade with the Dutch.
  • Evening: Take the ropeway up Mount Inasa (Inasayama) for one of Japan's "new three major night views."

Day 5: Travel to Hiroshima & Miyajima Island (April 11th, Saturday)

  • Morning: Check out of your Nagasaki accommodation. Take a train to Hiroshima (approx. 3.5-4 hours). Store your luggage at Hiroshima Station.
  • Afternoon: Take a ferry to Miyajima Island. Visit the iconic Itsukushima Shrine with its "floating" torii gate (check tide times for best viewing). Explore the island, perhaps hike a bit or see the deer.
  • Evening: Return to Hiroshima. Check into your accommodation. Enjoy Hiroshima-style Okonomiyaki for dinner.
  • Accommodation Options (per night for 2 people):
    • Cheap (AUD $70-100): Santiago Guesthouse Hiroshima, WeBase Hiroshima, Capsule Hotel & Sauna New Japan EX.
    • Normal (AUD $150-250): Hotel Granvia Hiroshima, Dormy Inn Hiroshima, Mitsui Garden Hotel Hiroshima.
    • Pricy (AUD $300-500+): Sheraton Grand Hiroshima Hotel, Rihga Royal Hotel Hiroshima, The Knot Hiroshima.
  • Dinner Options (per person):
    • Cheap (AUD $10-20): Basic Okonomiyaki from smaller shops, Momiji Manju (snack).
    • Normal (AUD $20-50): Okonomiyaki in Okonomi-mura, casual Anago Meshi, Izakayas with local dishes.
    • Pricy (AUD $50-100+): Specialty Oyster restaurants, high-end Anago Meshi on Miyajima, upscale Ryotei.

Day 6: Hiroshima Peace & History (April 12th, Sunday)

  • Morning: Visit the Peace Memorial Park and Atomic Bomb Dome. Spend time at the Peace Memorial Museum.
  • Afternoon: Explore Shukkei-en Garden, a beautiful traditional Japanese garden.
  • Evening: Enjoy dinner, perhaps trying some local oysters if in season.

Day 7: Travel to Kyoto & Gion Exploration (April 13th, Monday)

  • Morning: Check out of your Hiroshima accommodation. Take a Shinkansen to Kyoto Station (approx. 1.5-2 hours). Check into your accommodation.
  • Afternoon: Explore Gion, Kyoto's famous geisha district. Wander through its traditional streets, visit Yasaka Shrine, and enjoy the atmosphere.
  • Evening: Enjoy dinner in Gion or Pontocho Alley.
  • Accommodation Options (per night for 2 people):
    • Cheap (AUD $80-120): Piece Hostel Kyoto, The Millennials Kyoto, K's House Kyoto.
    • Normal (AUD $180-300): Hotel Gracery Kyoto Sanjo, Mitsui Garden Hotel Kyoto Shijo, Hotel Monterey Kyoto.
    • Pricy (AUD $350-700+): The Ritz-Carlton Kyoto, Four Seasons Hotel Kyoto, Hotel Okura Kyoto, traditional Ryokans like Tawaraya or Hiiragiya.
  • Dinner Options (per person):
    • Cheap (AUD $10-25): Ramen shops (e.g., Ichiran, Ippudo), street food in Nishiki Market (during opening hours), casual Udon/Soba.
    • Normal (AUD $25-70): Izakayas, restaurants specializing in Tofu dishes, Obanzai (Kyoto home cooking), more established Sushi/Tempura.
    • Pricy (AUD $70-200+): Kaiseki Ryori (multi-course traditional dinner), high-end Sushi/Tempura, fine dining with Kyo-ryori (Kyoto cuisine).

Day 8: Kyoto Temples & Gardens (April 14th, Tuesday)

  • Morning: Visit Fushimi Inari Shrine, famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates.
  • Afternoon: Explore Kiyomizu-dera Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage site with a stunning wooden stage offering panoramic views of Kyoto.
  • Evening: Enjoy dinner in the Pontocho area.

Day 9: Kyoto Culture & Late Blossoms (April 15th, Wednesday)

  • Morning: Visit Arashiyama Bamboo Grove and Tenryu-ji Temple.
  • Afternoon: Seek out late cherry blossom spots like Ninna-ji Temple (Omuro cherries) or Haradani-en Garden (private garden with diverse late bloomers).
  • Evening: Enjoy a special Kyoto dinner.

Day 10: Travel to Osaka & Dotonbori (April 16th, Thursday)

  • Morning: Check out of your Kyoto accommodation. Take a short train ride to Osaka (Shin-Osaka or Umeda/Namba, approx. 15-30 minutes). Check into your Osaka accommodation.
  • Afternoon: Head straight to Dotonbori and Namba. Soak in the vibrant atmosphere, see the Glico Man sign, and explore the bustling streets.
  • Evening: Indulge in Osaka's famous street food for dinner (Takoyaki, Kushikatsu).
  • Accommodation Options (per night for 2 people):
    • Cheap (AUD $80-120): Hostel World Osaka, The Dorm Hostel Osaka, Hotel Shin-Imamiya.
    • Normal (AUD $160-280): Hotel Gracery Osaka Namba, Hotel Hankyu RESPIRE Osaka, Cross Hotel Osaka.
    • Pricy (AUD $350-600+): The Ritz-Carlton Osaka, Conrad Osaka, St. Regis Osaka, InterContinental Osaka.
  • Dinner Options (per person):
    • Cheap (AUD $10-20): Takoyaki stalls, Kushikatsu eateries (Shinsekai), Butaman (551 Horai).
    • Normal (AUD $20-50): Okonomiyaki restaurants, Udon specialty shops, Negiyaki restaurants, Horumonyaki (Izakayas).
    • Pricy (AUD $50-100+): High-end Okonomiyaki/Teppanyaki, Fugu (Tecchiri) restaurants, upscale Izakayas.

Day 11: Osaka Castle & Shinsekai (April 17th, Friday)

  • Morning: Visit Osaka Castle and its surrounding park. Climb to the top for panoramic views.
  • Afternoon: Explore Shinsekai district, known for its retro charm, Tsutenkaku Tower, and many Kushikatsu restaurants.
  • Evening: Enjoy dinner in Shinsekai or explore another area like Umeda.

Day 12: Universal Studios Japan OR Day Trip to Nara (April 18th, Saturday)

  • Option 1: Universal Studios Japan: Spend a full day enjoying the rides and attractions, especially The Wizarding World of Harry Potter or Super Nintendo World.
  • Option 2: Day Trip to Nara: Take a short train ride to Nara to visit Todai-ji Temple (with its giant Buddha statue) and interact with the friendly wild deer in Nara Park.
  • Evening: Return to Osaka for dinner.

Day 13: Relaxing Spa Experience & Shopping (April 19th, Sunday)

  • Morning/Afternoon: Relaxing Day Spa Experience
    • Option 1 (Normal - AUD $50-100 per person): Spa World: Enjoy a full day at this multi-story onsen theme park with various themed baths and relaxation areas.
    • Option 2 (Pricy - AUD $100-200 per person): Solaniwa Onsen Osaka Bay Tower: Experience a traditional Japanese atmosphere with various baths, gardens, and dining options.
    • Option 3 (Luxury - AUD $200-400+ per person): Hotel Spa (e.g., Ritz-Carlton, Conrad): Indulge in high-end spa treatments and facilities for a more exclusive and tranquil experience.
  • Afternoon: Enjoy some last-minute shopping in areas like Umeda (department stores, Osaka Station City) or Shinsaibashi (covered shopping arcade).
  • Evening: Special Last Night Dinner.

Day 14: Special Last Day (April 20th, Monday)

  • Morning: Special Last Day Plan
    • Option 1 (Relaxed): Kuromon Ichiba Market & Breakfast: Start your day with a culinary adventure at "Osaka's Kitchen." Sample fresh seafood, local snacks, and enjoy a leisurely breakfast. Pick up some last-minute edible souvenirs.
    • Option 2 (Scenic): Umeda Sky Building Kuchu Teien Observatory: Visit this iconic observatory for breathtaking panoramic views of Osaka. It's a fantastic way to bid farewell to the city.
    • Option 3 (Cultural): Sumiyoshi Taisha Shrine: Visit one of Japan's oldest shrines, known for its unique architecture and serene atmosphere, offering a peaceful start to your last day.
  • Afternoon: Enjoy any last-minute activities or revisit a favorite spot.
  • Evening: Final dinner in Osaka.

Day 15: Departure (April 21st, Tuesday)

  • Morning: Enjoy a leisurely breakfast. Depending on your flight time, you might have time for some final souvenir shopping or a quick visit to a nearby park.
  • Late Morning/Early Afternoon: Head to Kansai International Airport (KIX) for your departure. Allow ample time for travel to the airport (approx. 1 hour by train from Namba/Umeda).

General Tips for Visiting Japan

  • Cash is King: While cards are accepted in major stores and hotels, many smaller shops, restaurants, and attractions are cash-only. Always carry enough yen.
  • Learn Basic Phrases: "Arigato gozaimasu" (thank you), "Sumimasen" (excuse me/sorry), "Konnichiwa" (hello) go a long way.
  • Etiquette:
    • Bowing: A common greeting and sign of respect.
    • Shoes: Remove shoes before entering homes, some restaurants, temples, and traditional accommodations.
    • Chopsticks: Don't stick them upright in rice, pass food with them, or point with them.
    • Tipping: Not customary and can be considered rude.
  • Public Transport: Japan's public transport is efficient and punctual. Google Maps is excellent for navigation.
  • Wi-Fi: Rent a pocket Wi-Fi device or buy a local SIM card for reliable internet access.
  • Convenience Stores: FamilyMart, 7-Eleven, and Lawson are everywhere and offer a wide range of food, drinks, and essentials.
  • Rubbish Bins: Public rubbish bins are scarce. Carry a small bag for your trash.
  • Tattoos: While becoming more accepted, tattoos can still be associated with Yakuza. You may be asked to cover them or denied entry to some public baths (onsen) or gyms.
  • Cherry Blossom Viewing: Be respectful of the trees and other viewers. Don't shake branches or pick blossoms.
  • Luggage Delivery Service (Takkyubin): Consider using this service to send your main luggage between hotels, especially when moving between cities. It's convenient and reliable.
